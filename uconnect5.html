Vehicle Infotainment

Stellantis × Harman International

UConnect 5 Infotainment System

UConnect 5 is Stellantis' next-generation in-vehicle infotainment system, designed for use across multiple Stellantis brands, including Maserati, Jeep, Fiat, and others. As part of the Harman UX/UI design team, I collaborated with designers, engineers, and vendors to deliver brand-specific themes, multilingual experiences, and production-ready visual assets for a vast ecosystem of screen sizes and vehicle categories.

The Challenge

One platform, dozens of brands, millions of drivers.

Stellantis needed a unified infotainment platform that could scale across multiple brands with distinct identities, dozens of vehicle models, different screen shapes and sizes, and global markets requiring multilingual support.

The system had to be visually cohesive, brand-authentic, and technically implementable across varied hardware environments — from entry-level Fiats to high-performance Maseratis.

Usability & Safety

Multi-Brand Themes

Distinct UI themes within a unified system architecture.

Each vehicle line required its own distinct UI theme, while maintaining the underlying UConnect system structure. This included color palettes, icon styles, background artwork, animations, and typefaces tailored to each brand's identity.

Because the themes would appear on multiple screen formats, we ensured that assets scaled seamlessly across various display sizes — from compact city car screens to large premium vehicle displays.

Asset Production & UX/UI

Production-ready UI artifacts optimized for automotive performance.

As part of the design team, I partnered with other designers to create production-ready UI artifacts, including custom iconography in Illustrator, startup loading animations in After Effects, and branded background images in Photoshop.

I also ensured that all assets were optimized for automotive performance — applying file size reductions, simplifying vector paths, and streamlining animations to meet the strict technical requirements of embedded vehicle systems.

Multilingual Support

A seamless experience for a global customer base.

We collaborated with a translation vendor to support a global audience across all regions where Stellantis vehicles are sold. This required careful attention to layout flexibility, typographic legibility, and icon universality.

To ensure a seamless experience for all regions, we adjusted layouts to accommodate long language strings, tested alignment and truncation behaviors, verified legibility across character sets including Latin and Cyrillic, and ensured that icons conveyed meaning independent of language.

Layout Flexibility

Adjusted UI layouts to accommodate varying string lengths across languages without breaking visual hierarchy.

Ensured all iconography conveyed clear meaning independent of language, supporting intuitive global use.

Truncation Behavior

Tested and refined text truncation patterns to gracefully handle edge cases in all supported languages.
