Internal OTA Site — FAQ Page

The FAQ page was designed to support Ford's internal Over-the-Air (OTA) Updates site. The goal was to make complex technical terminology more accessible and to help internal users quickly find answers to common questions. My role spanned research, content strategy, information architecture, and high-fidelity design.

The Challenge

Technical jargon was slowing engineers down — and there was nowhere to turn for help.

Our design team began by conducting user interviews and job shadowing sessions to understand how employees interacted with the OTA site during their daily workflows. Through this research, we discovered significant knowledge gaps around the technical language used — particularly with internal terminology such as ice packs, a core component of OTA updates.

These knowledge gaps slowed workflows and led to frequent support requests, creating friction for both engineers and the teams they reached out to for help.

No Central Resource

Users lacked a central place to find clear explanations of OTA processes, terms, and frequently asked questions.

Knowledge Gaps

Technical jargon created significant barriers to understanding, especially for newer team members onboarding to the platform.

Workflow Disruption

Unanswered questions led to frequent interruptions in daily workflows and a high volume of repetitive support requests.

Platform Integration

The FAQ page needed to feel native to the existing OTA site — not bolted on — to encourage regular use and trust.

Research & Insights

Card sorting and brainstorming sessions to organize content logically.

Our team set out to design an FAQ page that would directly address the issues we uncovered in discovery. We facilitated brainstorming sessions and applied card sorting methodologies to organize content logically, ensuring the FAQ would be intuitive to navigate for engineers at all levels of familiarity with the OTA system.

This research phase shaped both the content hierarchy and the visual design decisions that followed — particularly around how to surface the most critical terms and concepts without overwhelming users.

Information Gathering

Worked directly with engineers to define technical terms and acronyms in plain, accessible language that all users could understand.

Data Visualization

Incorporated visual aids and infographics for more complex concepts to reduce reliance on dense technical text alone.

User Interviews

Identified and prioritized the most frequently asked questions directly from user research sessions and job shadowing observations.

Card Sorting

Applied card sorting to validate content groupings and navigation structure before committing to high-fidelity design.

Tools & Process

Research-to-design, end to end.

The project spanned the full design process — from discovery and user research through wireframing, high-fidelity design, and usability testing — all within Ford's established internal design system to ensure seamless visual consistency with the broader OTA site.

The Solution

A validated FAQ page that measurably reduced user confusion.

We collaborated closely with the development team to verify technical accuracy and ensure all FAQ content aligned with internal standards. Every term definition and process explanation was reviewed and approved by engineering subject matter experts before publication.

Usability testing validated that the new page significantly improved understanding and reduced user confusion around OTA terminology. The page became a trusted reference point within the platform, reducing repetitive support requests and giving engineers a reliable, self-serve resource for the first time.

Reduced Support Requests

The self-serve FAQ page addressed the most common questions, freeing up support teams and reducing workflow interruptions.

Improved Comprehension

Usability testing confirmed measurable improvements in user understanding of OTA terminology and processes.
