Vehicle Infotainment

Harman International & Samsung

VisionNext — CES 2020

For the 2020 Consumer Electronics Show, Harman and Samsung partnered to develop a conceptual infotainment system for the Smart Fortwo vehicle. The resulting concept, VisionNext, was a fully functional prototype displayed live at CES 2020. I contributed to the UX/UI design, branding elements, and prototyping.

The Challenge

A high-impact, forward-looking concept under tight deadlines.

Harman needed an infotainment system that aligned with Harman and Samsung's innovation goals, reflected the Smart Fortwo's unique design language, and ran on a real-time prototype built under tight deadlines — all while remaining visually compelling on the CES show floor.

This project required rapid iteration, cross-functional collaboration, and physical prototyping to create a seamless digital and hardware experience.

Design Exploration

The hexagon as a visual bridge between digital and physical.

The Smart Fortwo features hexagon tessellations throughout its interior design — air vents, speaker grilles, and panel geometry. We used this signature shape as a foundation for the VisionNext interface, incorporating it into icon styles, button shapes, and background patterns.

This design approach created a visual bridge between the digital UI and the physical vehicle environment, making the experience feel cohesive and purposeful rather than a generic screen placed in a car.

My Contribution

Iconography, color palettes, and UI mock-ups.

As a visual designer, my role included creating iconography that drew from the hexagonal design system, establishing color palettes that felt fun and matched the energy of the Smart Fortwo, and UI mock-ups including the keyboard layout and home screen.

The final working prototype was displayed live on the CES 2020 show floor, demoed to automotive industry leaders, press, and general attendees.
